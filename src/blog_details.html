7 Things Every First-Time Homebuyer Should Know Before Applying For A Mortgage

Learn how to prepare for the mortgage process, improve approval chances, and secure the best possible interest rates.

June 3, 2026 • 8 Min Read • Michael Thompson

Buying your first home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll ever make. Understanding how mortgages work before applying can save you money, reduce stress, and help you make smarter decisions throughout the home-buying journey.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Check your credit score before applying
  • ✓ Save enough for a down payment
  • ✓ Compare lenders and mortgage options
  • ✓ Understand closing costs
  • ✓ Get pre-approved before house hunting

Understand Your Credit Score

Your credit score is one of the most important factors lenders evaluate when reviewing your mortgage application. A higher credit score can qualify you for lower interest rates, potentially saving thousands of dollars over the life of your loan.

Save For Your Down Payment

The size of your down payment affects your monthly mortgage payment and overall borrowing costs. While some programs allow lower down payments, saving more upfront often results in better loan terms.

"The best mortgage rate often goes to the borrower who prepares long before applying."

Compare Multiple Lenders

Don't settle for the first mortgage offer you receive. Compare rates, fees, and loan programs from multiple lenders to ensure you're getting the best deal available.
